Warm and frosty greetings to you all.

It's been some time since I've written a Christmas letter! Seeing as I've put this off past the 25th, I'll skip the post office and settle with the information superhighway. I give you the unfiltered update on my past year. I appologize for the length. I'll try to leave out all the boring, uninteresting parts. But no skimming, please. There will be a test.

January 2008 marked a new hobby: marathon running. It still seems like an mismatched activity for me and I'm sure my genetics wish I pursued something more sensible to my body-type. But with running, no matter how poorly you perform, as long as you finish the distance everybody will cheer for you and put a shiny medal around your neck! Clearly, I'm in it for the glory.

My first marathon was a 42 kilometer course which entered the four theme parks of Walt Disney World in Florida. This was the motivation I needed! All along the route were Disney characters and castmembers cheering on the 18,000 participants. I carried a camera with me, and noticed many others had the same idea. It was funny seeing people lined up for pictures with Mickey, in the middle of a race! A memorable moment for me was the final stretch in Epcot, where I ran  past full church choir singing their hallelujahs. The other big "wow" was running through the castle in Magic Kingdom with all guests behind ropes whooping and hollaring. What made this event even better was that Andrew and my friend Dan also trained for this race. Dan's wife, Emily, his little girl, Lily, and his mom tagged along for the Mickey magic. I'm glad they came too—not only were they great travel buddies, but when you have a little girl in your company, you can attend pretty much any attraction, no matter the targeted age group, without getting raised eyebrows!

Februrary was the harsh return to reality, but I'm so very thankful for the good job I have to come back to. In November of 2007, I left my position at Ambrose University College and joined Nonfiction Studios as a web developer on their team. It's a small web and print outfit of about 14 people and I feel really blessed scoring a sweet gig like this. I'm surrounded by talent and have been given so many opportunities to stretch and grow. I'm usually neck-deep in code but I love dev'ing in a creative environment! Also, six of us there have iPhones. So you know we're awesome.

March hit with a family trip to Seattle to witness my Uncle Roy finally getting married! (Oh, I bet he's tired of that old joke by now!) It was a fun weekend visiting old relatives again and welcoming in the new ones (welcome, Aunt Jeanine!). Getting home was a bit of an adventure at Sea-Tac. Our flight got bumped due to overbooking, so Andrew and I spent an extra 24 hours exploring every nook and cranny of that airport. In exchange for our troubles, we did receive free tickets and enough meal vouchers to feel like a hobo.

April wasn't notable, but May featured the next running event! It was just a small 10k run, but the whole family participated (Braden too!) and I did get a free shirt out of the deal. Andrew high-tailed it the whole way and earned second-place prizes. Second-place prizes tend to be corporate shwag. Lucky guy!

In June, my grandparents visited from Winnipeg just in time to see me turn twenty-six! My sister, Jessica, would argue the timing has something to do with her graduation from high school, but she thinks the world revolves around her. No, they were there for me. Me, me, me. There was a second graduation this summer where Andrew was invited to wear long flowing robes and get patted on the head by the Pink Bishop. Now that he's recieved his BA from St. Mary's, he's jumped on their brand new education program so that he may one day obtain his own classroom and transform into MR. SUDERMAN. You should see the beard he's grown for the part. It would frighten any child.

Summer was a blur for me. July here means the Calgary Stampede, and that's a busy time at work (we do their web). Also, the opening weekend of the Stampede marked my second full marathon!  This was another fun event with a course that cut through the Stampede grounds and maneuvered the pathways of the Calgary Zoo. Again, it was full family affair with everybody else completing the half marathon distance. In spirit of the Stampede, this race's finisher's medal doubles as a belt buckle. Once I claimed my new beltwear, I (sorely) waddled back to the Stampede grounds to watch the rodeo from some fancy seats while eating my after-run steak. All courtesy of the Calgary Stampede, seeing as we made their website so dang sexy. That was another great marathon day for me!

Two weeks later, Andrew flew away to New York to complete his second Ironman. I couldn't join him for that, but I heard it was rather soggy. The rest of us Sudermen kept active escaping to the mountains whenever a weekend hit. Ribbon Falls was particularily beautiful and seemingly perilous.

August was the month of visits. I can't remember feeling more popular. So... where's all my company now?! Oh well. I was thrilled to see Mark Levesseur and Andrew & Leanne Walton. Ah... friends from the Old Country (Winnipeg). Brock was also in town one weekend, but I totally forgot he was here. I'm still a little embarassed. Sorry buddy!

Also hailing from Winnipeg were my Aunt LaVonne and Uncle Andy with my cousins Jayden and Jenna. We dragged them up a mountain in Yoho National Park, which was pretty hilarious in hindsight. Flatlanders just aren't accustomed to our lumpy ways! We had a lot of fun and took time to explore Johnson Canyon and Lake Louise. I highly recommend a visit!

August was capped off with a road trip to Penticton, B.C. featuring Andrew, Braden and I. Ironman Canada was the big event that weekend and even though we had no one particular to cheer on, we set up camp on the sidewalk to shout niceties at the passers-by. The real purpose was to get Andrew's name on next year sign-up list. He was successful, and so was the weekend. Summer was finally over.

Autumn hit and with it came even more running! In September, Andrew, Braden, my Dad and I ran the Harvest Half Marathon. It's a local race which mostly weaves through Fish Creek Park. Then in October, the Suderman family met up with the Berkan family (minus two daughters) in Kelowna, B.C. There, we all ran the half distance in the Okanagan Marathon. Except for Andrew, mind, who thought he'd be smart and do two laps. Afterwards we visited the vineyards and enjoyed the extra summer weather the Okanagan offers.

Halloween was shared with Dan and Emily and their two girls. Yes, by this point, Lily had a new baby sister Bella! Although we only trick-or-treated a couple houses, costumes were worn and candy was consumed by all. I disguised myself as Andrew, which was easy since all his stuff is here.

By the end of November, Braden left the Suderman household and moved into his new digs in Cranston. Watching him go, it's tempting. Not Cranston, heavens no. But finally moving out on my own. I think leaving the nest will be difficult for me, seeing as I'm a guy who does not seek soltitude in a quiet apartment, but instead loves having family around, making noise and generating that "home" vibe. One might argue I could go out and procreate my own family. But that's whole other issue demanding attention!

This December has been cold but pleasant. I've been keeping myself busy with work and training for the next big run. Christmas was festive and spent with family (plus Braden). I've been keeping the house occupied for the week between the two stat holidays while the parents and Jessica warm up in California.

It's been a quiet week which has given me time to reflect on the year gone by. I've also had plenty of opportunity for nostalgia, mostly directed at all of you people. Annual Christmas letters are an excellent excuse to reconnect with friends & family. Fortunately for you, I have a reasonable excuse for you to reconnect back. You see, I would have preferred this letter was sent in the traditional manner, but even if I hadn't procrastinated, my address book is horribly lacking actual street addresses (for most of you anyway). So, if you don't think I'mtoo creepy, hit reply and send back your snail mail addy. Then, next year, I'll send one of these to your realmailbox! How exciting would that be?

Well that's all there's going to be. I hope this letter has found you all well and that your Chrismas was spent with those you love. Best wishes for 2009!
